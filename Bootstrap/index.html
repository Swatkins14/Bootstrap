Favourite TV Shows

A Guide to the who/what/where/when and why of my favourite TV shows




Doctor Who

The tales of a alien called The Doctor and his adventures in time and space.

  • 2005 - Current
  • Showrunners: Russell T Davies, Steven Moffatt, Chris Chibnall
  • BBC
Bojack Horseman

Washed-up TV star, Bojack Horseman, goes through the misfortunes of life and the bad choices made in response to them.

  • 2014 - 2020
  • Created by Raphael Bob-Waksberg
  • Netflix
Fleabag

A woman, known only as Fleabag, has no filter as she navigates life and love in London while trying to cope with tragedy.

  • 2016 - 2019
  • Created by Phoebe Waller-Bridge
  • BBC

I grew up watching Doctor Who from a young age so it's always been a show that I've had throughout different stages. But what means the most to me about this show was it was a great form of escapism and really allowed myself to immerse myself into such an amazing world.

Bojack Horseman was a show that when I first saw the trailer I mistook it for a lighthearted cartoon show that'd be a bit funny, I couldn't have been more wrong. This was a show that was so well written with such deep subject matter that at times I had to remind myself it was an animated show.

Fleabag was a show that had the rare trait of having a better second season that the first, though this isn't to say that the first season wasn't good, but just that the second was so fantatsic. The way the fourth wall breaks worked so well with the flow of the show was flawless, and how they incorporated this into the story was brilliant.
